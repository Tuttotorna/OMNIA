# Preventive Value Audit

## One-line claim

Do not blame downstream what was made probable upstream.

---

## Purpose

This audit tests whether a downstream harm was made probable by upstream design, incentives, defaults, price, distribution, availability, interface, architecture, or business model.

---

## Core distinction

A harm is not only the final event.

A harm may begin earlier, in the conditions that make the final event likely, profitable, normal, cheap, easy, scalable, or repeated.

---

## Audit questions

1. What downstream harm appears?
2. What upstream conditions made it more likely?
3. Who designed, priced, distributed, promoted, defaulted, or profited from those conditions?
4. Was the harm predictable?
5. Was the harm scalable?
6. Was prevention technically possible?
7. Was prevention economically disincentivized?
8. Who pays after the damage?
9. Who profits before the damage?
10. What upstream correction would reduce the harm?

---

## Scores

Use qualitative scores first.

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| Predictability | Could the harm be anticipated? |
| Scalability | Does the design reproduce the harm at scale? |
| Incentive | Is the harmful path profitable or convenient? |
| Avoidability | Could a safer design exist? |
| Externalization | Are costs shifted downstream? |
| Prevention leverage | Would upstream correction reduce downstream cost? |
| Repair burden | Who pays after the harm occurs? |

---

## Verdicts

| Verdict | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `UPSTREAM_CAUSAL_PRESSURE` | Upstream design or incentives materially increased the harm. |
| `DOWNSTREAM_ONLY` | No upstream design/incentive relation is shown. |
| `INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE` | The link is plausible but not established. |
| `PREVENTIVE_INTERVENTION_AVAILABLE` | A practical upstream correction exists. |
| `REQUIRES_POLICY_REVIEW` | The issue requires legal, regulatory, or institutional review. |

---

## Boundary

This audit does not prove individual innocence.

It does not remove personal responsibility.

It tests whether the downstream behaviour was made more probable by upstream design.

---

## Final statement

Do not blame downstream what was made probable upstream.\n
