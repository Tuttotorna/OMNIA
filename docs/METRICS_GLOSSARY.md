# Metrics Glossary

This glossary gives a public orientation to recurring OMNIA-style signals.

Exact implementation details may vary by module, protocol, or repository.

The glossary is not a replacement for source code or validation artifacts.

---

## Omega / structural coherence

A signal associated with structural coherence or admissibility under a declared measurement protocol.

Public meaning:

    how much structure appears preserved under the measured condition

Boundary:

    not semantic truth

---

## Omega-hat / residual invariance

A signal associated with what remains structurally invariant after transformation or perturbation.

Public meaning:

    what survives controlled change

Boundary:

    not final proof

---

## Drift

A signal associated with structural movement between states, variants, or transformations.

Public meaning:

    how much the structure moved

Boundary:

    not automatically failure

---

## SEI / saturation or exhaustion

A signal associated with diminishing marginal structural yield.

Public meaning:

    further transformation or analysis may no longer add useful structural information

Boundary:

    not a universal stopping law outside declared protocol

---

## IRI / irreversibility

A signal associated with non-recoverable structural loss under transformation.

Public meaning:

    some structural information appears not to return after perturbation

Boundary:

    not semantic contradiction by itself

---

## SCI / structural compatibility

A signal associated with compatibility between representations, variants, or structural states.

Public meaning:

    whether two measured structures remain mutually admissible

Boundary:

    not equivalence of meaning by itself

---

## Gate / boundary signal

A signal that indicates whether a measured process should continue, stop, flag, or require inspection.

Public meaning:

    measurement-level control signal

Boundary:

    not final decision authority

---

## General rule

Every metric should be read under the same boundary:

    measurement != inference != decision
