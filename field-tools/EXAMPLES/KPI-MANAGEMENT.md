# Example — KPI Management

## Decision D

Choose a management action.

## Object pi

KPI, dashboard number, target metric, average score.

## Field Omega

Operational system:

- workflow;
- incentives;
- constraints;
- dependencies;
- hidden costs;
- failure modes;
- downstream effects;
- future fragility.

## Failure mode

Two operational states can share the same KPI but require different actions.

`pi(omega1) = pi(omega2)`

but:

`D(omega1) != D(omega2)`

## Audit conclusion

If the KPI collapses action-relevant distinctions, the KPI cannot ground the management decision.

## Correction

Replace scalar KPI with a decision-specific field view.

## Final sentence

A KPI is valid only for the decisions it preserves.\n
