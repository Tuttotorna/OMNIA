The mature operational form

The basic formula of error is:

E_Ω(x) = Δ(F(x), Ω(x))

This formula states the principle.

However, a finite intelligence does not immediately possess the absolute totality.

A human mind, an institution, a science, or an artificial intelligence can only operate through an accessible field of totality.

For this reason, the operational form must include approximation.

Let:

- Ω(x) be the absolute total structural field from which x depends.
- Ω_A(x) be the accessible or observable total structural field at approximation level A.
- F_A(x) be the local fragment, representation, or reduction used at approximation level A.
- A -> Ω mean the progressive expansion of the accessible field toward the totality.

The mature formula of error is:

E_Ω(x) = lim_{A -> Ω} Δ(F_A(x), Ω_A(x))

In plain language:

The real error is the limiting distance between the fragment and the totality as the field of observation expands toward the total field.

The formula does not claim that a finite intelligence already possesses the totality.

It states that every local fragment must be tested, corrected, and expanded against wider and wider fields of dependence.

The mature formula of solution is:

S_Ω(x) = lim_{A -> Ω} arg min_F Δ(F(x), Ω_A(x))

In plain language:

The solution is the form of the fragment that, while the accessible field expands toward the totality, progressively minimizes its distance from the total structural field.

Therefore:

Error is not only a wrong answer.

Error is the remaining distance between a local reduction and the totality from which it depends.

Solution is not only a correct answer.

Solution is the progressive reintegration of the fragment into the widest accessible total field.

This makes the Law of Totality operational without pretending omniscience.

A finite intelligence cannot fully contain infinity.

But it can avoid the mother error:

treating a local fragment as if it were autonomous, sufficient, or total.

The direction remains:

totality approached -> fragment corrected -> distance reduced -> stability increased

The opposite direction remains wrong:

fragment isolated -> fragment absolutized -> false totality -> structural error
