# Measurement Boundary

OMNIA follows the boundary:

    measurement != inference != decision

This is not decorative.

It is the main safety and clarity rule of the engine.

---

## Measurement

Measurement means:

    produce a structural signal under declared conditions.

Examples:

- stability score;
- drift signal;
- irreversibility signal;
- compatibility signal;
- saturation signal;
- structural fragility flag.

---

## Inference

Inference means:

    interpret the signal.

Inference is not the same as measurement.

A measured instability can suggest fragility.

It does not automatically prove semantic falsehood.

---

## Decision

Decision means:

    act on the interpretation.

Decision is external.

OMNIA can inform decisions.

It does not own them.

---

## Allowed claims

OMNIA may claim:

- a structure was measured;
- a transformation was applied;
- a signal was emitted;
- a boundary was reached;
- a structure appeared stable or unstable under declared conditions;
- a result requires validation.

---

## Disallowed claims

OMNIA should not claim by itself:

- this is true;
- this is false;
- this is safe;
- this is insecure;
- this model is conscious;
- this cryptographic object is broken;
- this downstream decision is correct.

---

## Rule

The output must preserve the distinction:

    measured signal
    interpreted meaning
    external action
