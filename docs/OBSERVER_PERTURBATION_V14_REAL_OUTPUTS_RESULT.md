# OMNIA — V14 Real Output Stress Test (Local Model)

## Purpose

Evaluate OMNIA on **real LLM outputs** generated by a local model  
(`flan-t5-small`) without any manual construction or synthetic labels.

Goal:

> verify whether corrected_opi can surface structurally degraded outputs in a realistic setting.

---

## Setup

- Model: `google/flan-t5-small`
- Generation: local inference (no API)
- Samples: 60
- Prompts: mixed (math, QA, code, definitions, instructions)

Dataset:

data/real_llm_outputs_v14_local_flan_t5_small.jsonl

Runner:

examples/observer_perturbation_real_outputs_v14.py

---

## Method

For each output:

1. Apply OMNIA ObserverPerturbation lens
2. Compute:
   - raw_opi
   - corrected_opi
3. Rank all outputs by `corrected_opi`

No semantic labels are used.

Evaluation is **manual inspection of top-k**.

---

Examples of top-ranked outputs:

"25 1 0 13" "LDL" "9-12 + 1-12 11.5..." "B" "Replay A - A ("

Observed properties:

- malformed numeric sequences

---

## Observations

### 1. Strong signal on degraded outputs

Many top-ranked items are clearly:

- malformed
- incomplete
- non-coherent
- structurally unstable

This indicates that:

> corrected_opi is sensitive to structural degradation.

---

### 2. Not all failures are captured

Several clearly invalid outputs appear lower in the ranking:

- long incoherent paragraphs
- wrong answers with fluent structure
- pseudo-natural language noise

Also:

- some rows have `corrected_opi = 0`

This means:

> OMNIA does not detect all malformed outputs.

---

### 3. Behavior depends on output type

Corrected OPI tends to prioritize:

- short malformed outputs
- symbolic instability
- abrupt structural breaks

Less sensitive to:

- long but incoherent text
- fluent but semantically wrong answers

---

## Interpretation

This is **not** a semantic evaluation.

Correct interpretation:

> OMNIA acts as a structural instability signal, not a correctness detector.

More precisely:

- detects structural collapse
- partially surfaces degraded generations
- does not guarantee full recall

---

## Current Claim (Valid)

> On real local-model outputs, corrected_opi surfaces several structurally degraded generations in the top-ranked subset.

---

## Non-Claims

OMNIA does NOT:

- detect truth
- guarantee detection of all malformed outputs
- replace semantic evaluation
- act as a complete quality filter

---

## Limitations

- small local model (high noise baseline)
- no ground truth labels
- evaluation is manual (top-k inspection)
- corrected_opi is heuristic

---

## Value

Despite limitations, V14 demonstrates:

- real-world applicability
- no synthetic construction
- observable structural signal
- reproducible pipeline

---

## Conclusion

V14 confirms that:

> OMNIA can surface structural degradation in real LLM outputs.

But:

> it is a partial signal, not a complete detector.

---

## Next Step

Focus direction:

- improve recall on long incoherent outputs
- detect fluent but structurally inconsistent responses
- reduce zero-score blind spots
