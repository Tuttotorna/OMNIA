# Example — Preventive Design

## Decision D

Decide whether intervention should occur upstream or downstream.

## Object pi

Visible downstream behaviour or damage.

## Field Omega

Full harm-generating system:

- product design;
- pricing;
- interface;
- availability;
- marketing;
- default options;
- incentives;
- distribution;
- repairability;
- regulation;
- externalized costs.

## Failure mode

Two downstream behaviours may look similar, but one was made highly probable by upstream design while another was not.

`pi(omega1) = pi(omega2)`

but:

`D(omega1) != D(omega2)`

## Audit conclusion

If downstream observation collapses upstream causation, it cannot ground a fair intervention decision.

## Correction

Audit upstream probability-making conditions.

## Final sentence

Do not blame downstream what was made probable upstream.\n
